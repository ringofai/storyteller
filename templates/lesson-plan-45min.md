# Lesson Plan: 45-Minute Storytelling Session

A single-class-period introduction to storytelling structure. Suitable for secondary or university level.

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:
- Identify the three-act structure in a story
- Write a one-sentence hook
- Outline a simple story arc

## Materials

- Whiteboard or slides
- Printed handouts (or notebook paper)

## Lesson Flow

### Opening (5 min)

**Hook:** Play or read the first 30 seconds of a well-known short story or speech. Ask: "Why do you want to keep listening?" Write student responses on the board.

### Mini-Lecture: The Three-Act Structure (10 min)

Draw on the board:

```
  [Act 1: What Is]  →  [Act 2: The Gap]  →  [Act 3: New Bliss]
```

Define each act with one sentence. Use a movie everyone knows (e.g. Finding Nemo, Toy Story) as example.

### Exercise 1: One-Sentence Hook (5 min)

Instruct students:
1. Think of a story you want to tell (real or fictional)
2. Write the ending in one sentence
3. Write where your audience is now in one sentence
4. The gap is your hook

### Exercise 2: Three-Paragraph Outline (15 min)

Students write three paragraphs — one per act — using this template:

**Act 1:** "Once upon a time, [HERO] was [WHERE]."
**Act 2:** "Then one day, [INCIDENT]. The hero tried [ACTION]."
**Act 3:** "Finally, [RESOLUTION]. And that's why [POINT]."

### Pair Share (5 min)

Students share their hook + outline with a partner. Partner gives:
- One thing I love
- One question I have

### Closing (5 min)

Three volunteers share their one-sentence hook with the class. The class votes: "Which one makes you want to hear the full story?"

## Homework

Write the first 250 words of your story. Include one sensory detail.

## Extensions

- **With AI:** Students use the hook prompt to generate 3 alternative hooks
- **With LSP:** Students build a three-brick structure representing their three acts
- **Cross-language:** Hook is written in English, then adapted to Cantonese or Mandarin
