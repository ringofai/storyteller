# Case Study: Educator Training Workshop

## Context

**Participants:** Teachers and lecturers (various disciplines)
**Language:** English, Cantonese, Mandarin
**Duration:** 1-day intensive
**Focus:** How to teach storytelling with AI

## The Challenge

Educators know their students need storytelling skills — but they themselves were never taught how to teach it. They needed a framework they could use the next day, not a theory they'd have to translate into practice.

## The Approach

We ran the full 1-Day StoryCraft Intensive, with an additional **train-the-trainer layer**:

**Morning (Foundation):** Teachers experienced the framework as students — writing their own hooks, building structures, creating storyboards. They learned the framework by *doing* it.

**Afternoon (Pedagogy):** We switched from practitioner mode to facilitator mode. Teachers analysed:
- How would I adapt this for MY students' age/language/context?
- What AI prompts would work in MY classroom?
- How do I assess a story without crushing creativity?

## Key Modules Added for Educators

### Module: Assessment Without Destruction

How to give feedback that strengthens, not discourages:
- The "Three Loves / One Suggestion" structure
- Why scoring creativity is counterproductive (and what to score instead)
- How to use the rubrics without making storytelling feel like a checklist

### Module: AI in the Classroom

- Which AI prompts to give students (and which to keep for yourself)
- How to handle academic integrity concerns
- The difference between AI-assisted and AI-generated storytelling

### Module: Bilingual & Cross-Cultural Adaptation

Each teacher mapped their student population:
- What narrative structures do my students already know?
- Where do cultural expectations around storytelling differ from the Western three-act norm?
- How do I honour different storytelling traditions while teaching a shared framework?

## Teacher Takeaways

> "I've always known students need to tell better stories. Now I have a system to teach it."

> "The AI prompts are what I'll actually use. Not 'go use ChatGPT' but specific, stage-by-stage prompts."

> "The LEGO brick exercise was the unexpected star. I'm going to buy a set for my classroom."

## What We Learned

- **Teachers need a framework they can own, not a script they follow.** The StoryCraft Framework is designed to be adapted, not memorised.
- **The AI layer is the teacher's biggest concern and biggest opportunity.** Give them concrete prompts, not abstractions.
- **Train-the-trainer sessions work best when teachers experience the framework as students first.** They can't teach what they haven't felt.
