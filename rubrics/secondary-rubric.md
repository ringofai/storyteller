# Storytelling Rubric — Secondary Students (Ages 13-17)

Use this rubric to assess student stories across the StoryCraft Framework. For classroom use, focus on Stages 1 (Hook), 2 (Shape), and 5 (Land).

| Criterion | Emerging (1) | Developing (2) | Proficient (3) | Advanced (4) |
|:----------|:-------------|:---------------|:---------------|:-------------|
| **Hook** | Story has a beginning | Opening creates curiosity or interest | Opening creates a clear gap between "what is" and "what could be" | Opening is memorable, specific to the story, and emotionally resonant |
| **Structure** | Story has a beginning and an end | Story has beginning, middle, end with clear transitions | Three-act structure is visible and balanced | Structure is purposeful — each section serves the emotional arc |
| **Character** | A person or subject appears | The hero wants or needs something | The hero has a clear goal and faces an obstacle | The hero changes or learns something through the story |
| **Detail** | General statements only | One concrete detail or sensory moment | Uses metaphor OR sensory detail to make an abstract idea concrete | Uses multiple sensory details and metaphor without overwhelming the story |
| **Vocal Delivery** | Story is audible | Eye contact and pacing are attempted | Voice varies (pace, pitch, or volume changes) | Presence is confident; silence and emphasis are used deliberately |
| **Audience** | Story is told to the speaker | Speaker shows awareness of audience | Content and language are adapted to the audience | Engagement techniques (questions, eye contact, pauses) are used naturally |

## Scoring Guide

| Total Points | Level |
|:-------------|:------|
| 6-9 | Emerging — foundation stage. Focus on one clear structure. |
| 10-14 | Developing — structure is solid. Work on vocal delivery and detail. |
| 15-19 | Proficient — strong across all criteria. Experiment with vulnerability and metaphor. |
| 20-24 | Advanced — ready to tell this story to a real audience. |
